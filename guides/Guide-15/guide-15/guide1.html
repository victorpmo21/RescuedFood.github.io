Food waste in Panama

Food waste in Panama is a growing problem with serious environmental and social repercussions. Approximately one third of the food produced in the country is wasted, contributing to the depletion of natural resources and hunger. Although initiatives are being implemented to reduce waste, awareness and education are key to addressing this problem. Combating food waste is essential to ensure food security and protect the environment in Panama.

Food waste is a common problem in Panamanians homes and businesses alike. For householders, wasting food puts disposable income under pressure, especially at times of spiraling food price inflation.

Wasting food has impacts throughout commercial supply chains too, with producers struggling to meet demand, supermarkets trying to balance their food waste with keeping shelves stocked, and food waste disposal problems in hospitality, leisure and foodservice businesses.

Roughly, a sixth of the food we buy gets thrown away, including five million tonnes of potentially edible food.

At home, you can reduce food waste by being sensible about what you buy and use. Resist multi-buy offers if you will not use the product in time.

Freeze ingredients if you can, if you are not going to use them before they go off.

More simple steps to cut food waste at home:

- Plan your meals to use up older ingredients first.
- Only cook as much as you will eat (you can always have dessert!).
- Check if food has spoiled – do not just bin it on its best before date.

In a commercial setting, the same rules apply. Adopt a FIFO (First In, First Out) strategy when putting ingredients in your freezer room or chiller – use the oldest ingredients first.

Be creative with your specials board. There are lots of ways to use up ingredients by offering a chef’s special, and regular diners might appreciate the variety.
