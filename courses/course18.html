Course Overview

Sustainability and organic foods are related to agricultural and food practices that seek to minimize environmental impact, promote soil health, and offer healthier products. Sustainability in food involves considering the complete life cycle of food, from production to consumption and waste management, with the aim of reducing the ecological footprint.

Organic foods are grown without the use of pesticides and synthetic fertilizers, and adhere to more sustainable agricultural practices. These products are often produced using methods that respect the environment, promote biodiversity, and seek to maintain soil health.

Both concepts, sustainability and organic foods, reflect a growing concern for the health of the planet and people, promoting more responsible agricultural and consumer practices.
