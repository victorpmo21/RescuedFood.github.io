Course Overview

Food handling refers to safe and appropriate practices for the preparation, storage, transportation and serving of food, with the objective of preventing contamination and ensuring food safety. This includes hygienic handling of ingredients, proper separation of raw and cooked foods, storage at safe temperatures, thorough cooking of raw foods, and personal hygiene of food handlers. Proper food handling is essential to prevent foodborne illnesses and ensure the quality of food products from their origin to final consumption. These practices are applicable both in domestic environments and in the food industry and are regulated by food safety standards established by health authorities.
