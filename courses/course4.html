Course Overview

Animal feeding refers to the provision of foods specifically designed to meet the nutritional needs of animals, whether in domestic environments or in agricultural production. These feeds are formulated to provide essential nutrients, such as proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins and minerals, that are necessary for the growth, development and maintenance of health of animals. Animal feed can vary depending on the type of animal, its age, its species and its purpose, whether it is pets, livestock, poultry or other farm animals. The animal feed industry strives to develop balanced and nutritious diets to ensure animal welfare and, in some cases, improve efficiency in animal production.
