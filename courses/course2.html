Course Overview

Composting is a natural process of breaking down organic materials, such as kitchen and garden scraps, to create an organic fertilizer called compost. During composting, decomposer microorganisms, such as bacteria and fungi, break down organic matter into nutrient-rich material that can be used to improve soil quality. This process helps reduce the amount of organic waste sent to landfills, contributes to environmental sustainability and promotes soil health by increasing its nutrient content and improving its structure. Composting is a common practice both at home and at the community or agricultural level.
